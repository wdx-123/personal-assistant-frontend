I have identified the cause of the sidebar "vibration" (layout shifting). It is caused by the conflict between the smooth CSS `width` transition and the instant `justify-content` property change.

When the sidebar collapses:
1.  **Header & Menu**: `justify-content` instantly switches to `center` while the width is still animating, causing elements to jump to the calculated center of the *current* width (240px) and then slide left, creating the "left-right" jitter effect.
2.  **Footer**: The `gap` property is not transitioned, causing a small layout shift (approx 5px) when the text disappears.

**Implementation Plan**:

1.  **Fix Sidebar Header (`.sidebar-header`)**:
    *   Remove `justify-content: center` and `padding: 0` from the `.collapsed` state.
    *   Instead, maintain the existing `padding-left: 16px` (which perfectly centers the 32px logo in the 64px collapsed sidebar).
    *   *Result*: The logo will stay anchored to the left padding, which naturally becomes "centered" as the container shrinks to 64px.

2.  **Fix Menu Items (`.menu-title`)**:
    *   Remove `justify-content: center` from the `.collapsed` state.
    *   Update the `.collapsed` padding to `0 14px` (calculated to center the 20px icon in the 48px content area).
    *   *Result*: The icon will smoothly slide from 12px padding (expanded) to 14px padding (collapsed), eliminating the jump.

3.  **Fix Sidebar Footer (`.sidebar-footer`)**:
    *   Add `gap` to the `transition` property list.
    *   Add `gap: 0` to the `.collapsed` state.
    *   *Result*: The gap between the icon and the (hidden) text will close smoothly, keeping the icon perfectly centered.
